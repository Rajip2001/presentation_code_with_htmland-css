Definition of Research Methodology:

Research methodology refers to the systematic way of solving a research problem. It encompasses various techniques and procedures used to conduct research.

Importance of Tools and Instruments in Research:

Tools and instruments are crucial as they enable researchers to collect data effectively, ensuring the reliability and validity of findings.

Types of Research Instruments

Primary vs. Secondary Instruments:

Primary Instruments: Directly collect data from participants (e.g., surveys, interviews).

Secondary Instruments: Utilize existing data sources (e.g., literature reviews, databases).

Examples:

Questionnaires: Structured sets of questions used to gather quantitative or qualitative data from respondents.

Interviews: Conversational tools used to gather in-depth qualitative data from participants.

Observations: Systematic recording of behaviors or phenomena in natural or controlled settings.

Characteristics of Good Research Instruments

Validity:

Ensures that the instrument measures what it intends to measure.

Reliability:

Consistency and stability of measurement results.
