Definition of Research Methodology:

• Research methodology refers to the systematic way of solving a research problem.

• It encompasses various techniques and procedures used to conduct research.

• Research methodology involves systematic investigation, analysis, and interpretation of information to advance knowledge.

• Research tools and instruments refers to the techniques or strategies used to gather, analyze and interpret data in research studies.

Characteristics of Good Research Instruments

• Must gather data suitable for and relevant to the research topic

• Able to test hypothesis and/or answer proposed research questions under investigation

• Free of bias and appropriate for the context, culture, and diversity of the study site

• Contains clear and definite instructions to use the instrument

• Valid and reliable

• Based on a conceptual framework, or the researcher's understanding of how the particular variables in the study connect with each other
